\section{Recent Professional Disasters}

\subsection{November 2024 — Present: Cloud Architect at LBBW}
\textbf{The "Skywalker" Platform Awakening}

Joined LBBW's Phoenix project expecting to modernize IT for Germany's major state bank. Reality: Skywalker platform exists but isn't used in production. My job involves cataloging hundreds of legacy applications and pretending the R-methodology will magically solve decades of technical debt.

\textbf{Learning Moments:}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*,topsep=0pt,itemsep=2pt]
\item Discovered that banking IT moves at geological speeds
\item Learned that "cloud-ready" means "theoretically possible but practically terrifying"
\item Realized that enterprise architecture is 90\% PowerPoint presentations
\item Found out that "migration assessment" is corporate speak for "let's think about it"
\end{itemize}

\subsection{October 2021 — December 2024: Cloud Architect at Union Investment}
\textbf{The B2B Portal That Ate My Soul}

What started as a "simple portal modernization" became a 3-year odyssey through the depths of enterprise architecture hell. Built microfrontends before realizing most developers don't know what Web Components are.

\textbf{Epic Failures:}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*,topsep=0pt,itemsep=2pt]
\item Spent 6 months building a microfrontend toolkit that 3 people use
\item Created event-driven architecture for 8 domains that nobody asked for
\item Implemented 1000+ tests that take 10 minutes to run because "comprehensive testing"
\item Built search functionality that works perfectly for the 80,000 users who don't use it
\item Served as Product Owner while having no product management training
\item Introduced Self-contained Systems pattern to teams who preferred monoliths
\end{itemize}

\textbf{360° Feedback Reality Check:} Team consistently rated my technical skills 10/10 while politely suggesting I explain things in "human language" sometimes. Apparently, "just implement an event-driven SCS architecture" isn't helpful guidance.

\subsection{December 2021 — November 2024: Middleware Engineer at Saloodo!}
\textbf{The Golang Loneliness Project}

Took this part-time gig to learn Golang professionally. Became the sole maintainer of critical middleware syncing 10 million records between broken databases and Salesforce. Achieved 100\% uptime mostly because I was too afraid to touch anything.

\textbf{Survival Lessons:}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*,topsep=0pt,itemsep=2pt]
\item Learned that "fault-tolerant" means "accepting that everything is broken"
\item Discovered that 70\% test coverage means 30\% pure panic
\item Realized that being the only developer means you're also the only one to blame
\item Found that malformed JSON in production databases is more common than you'd think
\end{itemize}

\subsection{August 2018 — October 2019: Cloud Consultant at Trusted Shops}
\textbf{The AWS Lambda Learning Curve}

Modernized subscription systems while learning that serverless isn't actually serverless – it's just someone else's servers that you can't debug.

\textbf{Humbling Moments:}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*,topsep=0pt,itemsep=2pt]
\item Spent weeks optimizing Micronaut for Lambda cold starts
\item Built comprehensive Terraform modules that nobody maintains
\item Introduced contract testing that everyone agrees is "great in theory"
\item Migrated from JavaEE6 monolith to microservices, creating 13 new problems
\end{itemize}
